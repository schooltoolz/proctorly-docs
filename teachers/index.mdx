Proctorly gives you a complete workflow for running browser-based proctored exams — from setting up a test in under two minutes to reviewing per-student integrity reports after everyone has submitted. Nothing needs to be installed, and your students do not need a Proctorly account.

The teacher workflow follows four stages. First, you create a test by linking a Google Form or uploading a PDF, Excel, or Word file. Proctorly generates a unique secure token, and the student access URL is ready to share immediately. Second, you invite students by email; each student receives a personalized link that only works for them. Third, while the exam runs, the live monitoring dashboard shows every active session in real time, surfaces flag counts and risk levels, and lets you broadcast messages, approve pause requests, or lock all screens at once. Fourth, once students submit, you review the post-exam report: a per-session flag timeline, integrity scores from 0–100, webcam snapshot galleries, and on-demand AI-generated summaries.

The pages in this section cover each stage in detail. If you are new to Proctorly, start with [Creating a test](/teachers/creating-a-test) and work through the guide in order.
