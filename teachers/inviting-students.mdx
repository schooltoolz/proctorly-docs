---
title: "Inviting students to take their proctored exam session"
sidebarTitle: "Inviting students"
description: "Add student email addresses so Proctorly sends each person a unique personalized exam link. Students need no Proctorly account to access their exam."
---

Proctorly sends every invited student a unique link tied to their email address. When a student opens their link, Proctorly identifies them without requiring an account or sign-in. Each link is personalized — students cannot use each other's links, and the exam session is recorded against the correct student from the moment they click through.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the test's invitation panel">
    From your **Tests** page, click the test you want to invite students to. On the test detail page, open the **Invite students** section.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter student email addresses">
    Type or paste student email addresses, one per line or separated by commas. You can invite multiple students at once.

    <Warning>
      Invite limits apply based on your plan: Free allows up to 15 students per test, Classroom up to 30, and School has no cap. Excess addresses beyond your plan limit will be rejected.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Send invitations">
    Click **Send invitations**. Proctorly emails each student a message with a **Start assessment** button. The button links to a URL unique to that student — for example:

    ```text
    https://proctorly.schooltoolz.com/t/a1b2c3d4e5f6?s=<student-token>
    ```

    The subject line includes your test title so students can identify the email easily.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What the invitation email contains

Each student's email includes:

- Your name as the inviting teacher
- The test title
- A **Start assessment** button linking to their personalized URL
- A reminder that they need a working webcam and a stable internet connection
- A note that the link is unique to them and must not be shared

Students do not need a Proctorly account. They open the link, enter their name, and proceed through the consent and camera setup steps before the exam begins.

## Sharing the token URL directly

If you prefer to distribute access another way — for example, through your LMS or a class message — you can copy the base test URL (`proctorly.schooltoolz.com/t/<token>`) and share it yourself. Students who arrive via the generic token URL will be asked to enter their email address before their session begins, and Proctorly will match them to an existing invitation or create an anonymous session depending on your test settings.

<Tip>
  If a student loses their invitation email, you can resend it from the test detail page, or copy their unique personalized link from the student list and send it manually.
</Tip>

## Returning students

If a student opens their personalized link a second time — for example, after being disconnected mid-exam — Proctorly reconnects them to their existing session rather than starting a new one. Their session history and any flags recorded before the disconnect are preserved.
