ID verification is an optional feature that some teachers enable on their exams. If it is turned on, you will be asked to take a quick photo of a government-issued or school-issued ID immediately after you submit your exam. This lets your teacher confirm that the person who took the exam matches the name and email on the session record. If your teacher has not enabled ID verification, this step will not appear.

## When it appears

The ID verification prompt appears on the completion screen, right after you submit your exam. Proctorly displays a webcam frame and a **Capture photo** button. You will not be asked for your ID before or during the exam — only after submission.

<Note>
  If you do not see an ID verification prompt after submitting, your teacher has not enabled this feature for your exam. You are done.
</Note>

## How to take the photo

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare your ID">
    Get out your ID before you click **Capture photo**. Government-issued photo ID (passport, driver's license, national ID card) and school-issued student ID cards are all typically accepted. Check with your teacher if you are unsure which ID is acceptable.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Position your ID in front of the camera">
    Hold the ID horizontally about 20–30 cm (8–12 inches) from your webcam. Center it within the preview frame on screen. Make sure the full card — including all four corners — is visible inside the frame.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check the photo conditions">
    Before clicking **Capture photo**, verify:

    - The text and photo on the ID are in focus and readable.
    - The room is well lit — a bright light source in front of you (not behind you) prevents glare and shadows.
    - There is no glare or reflection covering the ID number, name, or photo.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Capture the photo">
    Click **Capture photo**. Proctorly takes a still frame from your webcam. A preview of the captured image will appear.

    If the image is blurry, dark, or does not show the full card, click **Retake** to try again. There is no limit on retakes.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit your photo">
    When the preview looks clear, click **Submit photo**. The image is uploaded to your session record, and you will see the exam completion screen. You can now close the browser tab.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips for a good ID photo

<Tip>
  Face a window or lamp so the light falls on the front of the ID. Avoid sitting with a bright window behind you — backlighting makes the ID appear dark and hard to read.
</Tip>

- Hold the ID steady and flat — tilting it at an angle causes the text to become distorted.
- Remove the ID from any plastic sleeve or wallet slot before capturing. Plastic can create reflective glare.
- If the webcam has trouble focusing, try moving the ID slightly closer or further away until the preview looks sharp.
- Make sure your hands do not cover any part of the ID when you hold it up.

## What your teacher sees

After you submit your photo, it is attached to your session record in the Proctorly report. Your teacher can open the report, find your session, and view the ID photo alongside your name, email, and session flags. Proctorly does not run any automated ID matching — your teacher reviews the photo manually and makes their own determination.

<Note>
  ID photos are stored securely and are only visible to the teacher who administered the exam. They are subject to the same data retention policy as the rest of your session record.
</Note>
