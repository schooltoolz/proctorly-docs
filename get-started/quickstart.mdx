---

This guide walks you through the complete Proctorly workflow from sign-up to post-exam review. Students need no Proctorly account — they access the exam through the unique URL you share. You can have a live proctored test ready in under ten minutes.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up at proctorly.schooltoolz.com">
    Go to [proctorly.schooltoolz.com](https://proctorly.schooltoolz.com) and create an account. You can sign up with Google, Microsoft, or an email address and password.

    After signing in you land on the teacher dashboard. No credit card is required to start — the Free plan includes 2 proctored tests per month with up to 15 students each.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a test">
    Click **New Test** from the **My Tests** page. Give the test a title, set an optional time limit, and choose your exam source.

    Proctorly supports two exam source types:

    - **Google Forms URL** — paste a `docs.google.com/forms` link. Proctorly embeds the form inside the proctored session so students never leave the monitored window.
    - **File upload** — upload a PDF, Excel (.xlsx), or Word (.docx) file up to 10 MB. Proctorly stores the file securely and serves it inside the session.

    <Note>
      File uploads are limited to 10 MB. For larger exams, use a Google Forms URL instead.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add student emails to invite">
    On the test settings page, enter your students' email addresses in the **Invited students** field. Proctorly uses this list to match sessions to known students and pre-fill names during the student setup flow.

    Inviting students is optional — anyone with the exam link can start a session. Adding emails lets you track attendance and associate sessions with specific students in the integrity report.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Share the exam link">
    After creating the test, Proctorly generates a unique token URL:

    ```
    https://proctorly.schooltoolz.com/t/<token>
    ```

    Copy this link and share it with your students via email, your LMS, or a class chat. Students do **not** need a Proctorly account to open it.

    <Warning>
      Keep the token link private. Anyone with the link can start a session. If you need to prevent new students from joining, set the test status to **Closed** in the test settings.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Monitor the live dashboard">
    While the exam is running, open the test from **My Tests** and click **Live Monitoring**. You can see each active session, view webcam snapshots, and watch flags appear in real time.

    Proctorly captures webcam and screen snapshots every 3 minutes as a baseline, plus an immediate snapshot whenever a flag fires.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the integrity report">
    Once a student submits, click **View Report** on the test. Each student session shows:

    - A **flag timeline** with timestamps for every detected event
    - **Webcam and screen snapshots** attached to flag events
    - An **AI-generated integrity summary** that explains the findings in plain language
    - Verdict controls to mark the session **Clear**, **Flag for Review**, or **Escalate**

    <Tip>
      The AI summary is generated by Claude Haiku. It synthesizes all detected events into a short paragraph — useful for making fast verdict decisions without reviewing every timestamp manually.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>
